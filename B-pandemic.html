We can see in the above graph that even before pandemic E-commerce companies used to dominate the indian market and almost always used to be in the top 10 most profitable companies in India like Amazon, big-basket, grofers, apple etc. This shows the power and efficiency of doing trade through e-commerce. Online Selling companies

Amazon

Amazon is a buisness giant heavily dependent on e-commerce for its revenue.
It is a goods providing company that provides essentials at door step. Due to it being dependent on ecommerce, it has grown tremendously to whole world.
Its CEO, Jeff Bezos was recently the world's richest for a brief period and now is ranking no. 2 in the world's richest people.
Amazon has always been one of the most profitable companies in India. It has grown exponentially from earning a meager profit of 6.92 billion to 280.52 billion dollars before pandemic.

This shows that E-commerce is so beneficial that is also brings employement to many people adding to its never ending list of specialities and advantages.
During pandemic the numbers have risen shariply as many people got unemployed due to small companies firing them due to insufficient revenue due to being in buisness offline. This increase in employement rate has brought in tremendous amounts of revenue as well as has helped millions to get an income.
We should really be thankful for ecommerce.

PayTM

PayTM is a digital payment system and financial technology company. It is one of the most successful companies in India and is now setting up its roots in other countries. It has an easy to use app that allows a user to make seamless transactions in seconds.

Revenue from operations and net worth had touched an all time high in 2019-20. Its revenue from operations also improved marginally to ₹3,115 crore in 2019-20 from ₹3,049.87 crore in the previous fiscal. Its net worth had increased by 39.35 per cent to ₹8,411 crore as on March 31, 2020.

We can see that during the pandemic PayTM didn't get affected that much as it was already dependent on e-commerce. But as we can see that PayTM considerable reduction in terms of losses hence increasing the overall revenue. This was so because that it previously had been about 80-90% online and 10-20% offline. But after pandemic the situation changed due to lockdown with people heavily dependent on the PayTM app for transactions.

We can see in this data that PayTM is the most preffered single mode of digital payment and transaction. Its easy to use app, features and availability at every local shop make it a convinient method of payment and attracts users hence making its user base even larger.
